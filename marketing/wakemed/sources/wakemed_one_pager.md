### The problem you already know

Inadequate onboarding accounts for **35% of first-year nurse departures**. Replacing one bedside nurse costs ~$52K. Time-to-independent-competency is 6–12 months. The national workforce shortage is projected to cost $917B by 2030. WakeMed isn't immune — and slide-deck-plus-shadowing isn't moving the curve.

### What we built

**AIWIZN is an AI-driven scenario engine that puts new nurses inside the decisions that matter** — STEMI activation under hypotension, tPA candidacy with consent crises, sepsis bundle execution under pressure. The engine reads the patient state machine, captures every decision via a stealth assessment layer (Bayesian knowledge networks, NBME-validated methodology), and produces a placement-ready competency profile. Live monitor traces. Real-time pedagogy reveal after each decision. No slide decks.

It's already running. Try it: **[demo.aiwizn.com](https://demo.aiwizn.com)**.

### Why WakeMed first

Graham has been the clinical anchor for this work since v1. Every scenario in the cardiac, stroke, and sepsis modules has been built against WakeMed-grade clinical standards. The engine is **multi-tenant by design** — WakeMed's door-to-balloon targets, standing-order vocabulary, scope-of-practice rules, and branding all configure independently of the engine code. When a WakeMed nurse logs in, they see WakeMed protocols, not a generic textbook.

The pilot is wired up. Diane and Robin can log in this week.

### Why this is research, not procurement

We're proposing a **co-designed, IRB-pathway investigation**: control cohort runs WakeMed's current onboarding pathway, intervention cohort augments with AIWIZN. Primary endpoint: time-to-competency. Secondary: knowledge retention at 6 months, first-year retention. Pre-registered protocol, Graham as senior author, publication target in *Simulation in Healthcare* or *Nurse Educator*. Both sides own the data; WakeMed owns the institutional learning.

If the engine works, you have a published validation paper. If it doesn't, you've protected the cohort and learned something definitive. Either way, you don't carry vendor risk.
