## 1. Purpose

This MOU establishes a research collaboration between AIWIZN and WakeMed to evaluate the AIWIZN scenario-based clinical training platform in onboarding newly hired registered nurses, with the joint objective of (a) characterizing the platform's effect on time-to-competency, knowledge retention, and 12-month retention, and (b) co-authoring a peer-reviewed publication of the findings.

## 2. Scope of collaboration

- WakeMed enrolls newly hired RNs into the study cohorts per the attached **Research Study Protocol** (separate document, version-controlled jointly)
- AIWIZN provides platform access at no cost during the pilot, configured to WakeMed's protocols (door-to-balloon ≤75 min, door-to-needle ≤45 min, WakeMed-specific standing-orders, branding, terminology)
- AIWIZN provides telemetry data and statistical-analysis support
- WakeMed Education provides preceptor sign-off records, baseline time-to-competency data, and operational coordination
- Dr. Graham Snyder serves as Senior Author on resulting publications and as Medical Reviewer for any AIWIZN clinical content updates during the study window

## 3. Pricing and consideration

This is a **research collaboration**, not a commercial license sale. During the 14-month pilot:
- WakeMed pays a nominal **$_____** to AIWIZN (covering platform configuration, support, and statistical-analysis services; well below standard AIWIZN pilot pricing)
- AIWIZN receives access to anonymized aggregate outcome data for product validation and publication
- Both parties have publication rights to the joint findings

Post-pilot commercial terms (if WakeMed wishes to continue) are addressed in a separate licensing agreement to be negotiated in good faith at month 12 of the pilot.

## 4. Data ownership and handling

- **WakeMed's institutional data** (cohort identities, preceptor records, HR turnover data) remains the property of WakeMed and is not transferred to AIWIZN
- **Platform telemetry** (decision selections, scoring profiles, time-on-task) is stored in AIWIZN's Supabase with WakeMed-scoped RLS and accessible to both parties
- **Aggregate findings** (statistical summaries, anonymized analyses) are jointly owned and may be published by either party with co-author credit to the other
- **No PHI** is transferred under this MOU; all scenario content is synthetic
- AIWIZN agrees to standard Business Associate Agreement terms if any inadvertent PHI exposure arises (no PHI is anticipated)

## 5. IRB and regulatory

- WakeMed serves as the IRB of record (its IRB office reviews and approves the protocol)
- AIWIZN cooperates fully with any IRB queries and provides platform documentation as requested
- Either party may suspend the study pending IRB or regulatory review without breach

## 6. Intellectual property

- AIWIZN owns and retains all rights to the AIWIZN platform, engine code, scoring methodology, and clinical scenarios
- WakeMed owns and retains all rights to its operational data, preceptor rubrics, and institutional methodology
- Joint findings, aggregate analyses, and the resulting manuscript are jointly owned with equal author and publication rights
- Neither party may use the other's name or trademarks in marketing materials without prior written consent, with the exception of factual references to this pilot in research, regulatory, or publication contexts

## 7. Publication terms

- Target submission window: within 14 months of IRB approval
- Senior author: Dr. Graham E. Snyder, MD FACEP
- Co-authorship rights extend to any individual at either organization who meets ICMJE authorship criteria
- Both organizations have right of first review of any draft manuscript prior to submission
- Neither party may unilaterally publish primary outcome data without the other's reasonable consent (consent not to be unreasonably withheld; 30-day review window applies)

## 8. Confidentiality

- Each party will treat the other's non-public information as confidential under the terms of the existing mutual NDA (or, if none, the standard mutual NDA attached as Exhibit A)
- Aggregate study findings are NOT confidential once approved for publication
- Underlying institutional data (WakeMed) and platform internals (AIWIZN) remain confidential indefinitely

## 9. Termination

Either party may terminate this MOU with **30 days written notice**, without cause. On termination:
- AIWIZN provides WakeMed with a final telemetry export
- Data already collected may be used by either party for the contractually-permitted publication
- No further fees are owed
- All confidentiality, IP, and publication terms survive termination

## 10. Limitations of liability

Each party's total liability under this MOU is capped at the pilot fee paid. Neither party is liable for consequential, indirect, or punitive damages. Standard mutual indemnification for third-party IP claims applies.

## 11. Governing law

This MOU is governed by the laws of the State of North Carolina. Disputes will be resolved by good-faith negotiation, then by binding arbitration in Wake County, NC.
